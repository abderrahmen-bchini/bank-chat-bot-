# Wifak Bank - Employee Leave Policy

**Document Reference:** HR-POL-004  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026  
**Version:** 3.2  
**Owner:** Human Resources Department  

---

## 1. Overview
At Wifak Bank, we recognize the importance of work-life balance and are committed to providing a comprehensive leave program that supports our employees' health, personal obligations, and well-being. This policy outlines the types of leave available, eligibility criteria, accrual rates, carry-over rules, and the approval process.

---

## 2. Types of Leave

### 2.1 Annual Leave (Paid Time Off)
* **Eligibility:** All full-time and part-time permanent employees.
* **Accrual Rate:** Permanent full-time employees accrue annual leave at a rate of **2.08 days per month** worked, resulting in a total of **25 days** of paid annual leave per calendar year. Part-time employees receive pro-rata leave based on their contracted hours.
* **Carry-over Rules:** 
  * Employees are encouraged to take all their annual leave within the calendar year.
  * A maximum of **5 days** of unused annual leave may be carried over into the next calendar year.
  * Any carried-over leave must be fully utilized by **March 31st** of the new year, or it will be forfeited without financial compensation.
* **Request and Approval:**
  * Leave requests must be submitted through the internal HR Portal (*PeopleConnect*) at least **two weeks (14 days)** prior to the start of the requested leave period.
  * Line managers must approve or decline requests within 3 business days, considering team operational coverage.

### 2.2 Sick and Medical Leave
* **Eligibility:** All permanent employees from their first day of employment.
* **Entitlement:** Employees are entitled to up to **10 days** of fully paid sick leave per calendar year for personal illness or injury.
* **Medical Certification:**
  * For any sickness absence exceeding **3 consecutive business days**, a valid medical certificate from a registered healthcare professional must be submitted to HR.
  * Self-certification is permitted for absences of 1 to 3 days, up to a maximum of 3 occurrences per year.
* **Extended Medical Leave:** If an employee is unable to work due to long-term illness, they may transition to the Short-Term Disability (STD) program, which covers 70% of base salary for up to 26 weeks.

### 2.3 Parental and Family Leave
Wifak Bank offers generous parental leave options to support new parents:
* **Maternity Leave:** Eligible birthing parents are entitled to **18 weeks** of fully paid leave.
* **Paternity / Partner Leave:** Non-birthing parents, including adoptive parents, are entitled to **8 weeks** of fully paid leave.
* **Adoption Leave:** Primary adoptive parents are eligible for **12 weeks** of fully paid leave.
* **Notice Requirement:** Employees must notify HR and their manager in writing at least **10 weeks** before the expected due or placement date.

### 2.4 Compassionate and Bereavement Leave
* **Entitlement:** Employees may take up to **5 consecutive business days** of paid leave in the event of the death or critical illness of an immediate family member (spouse, partner, child, parent, sibling).
* **Extended Family:** Up to **3 days** of paid leave is granted for extended family members (grandparent, parent-in-law).
* **Requests:** Should be communicated verbally or in writing to the line manager as soon as practicable, followed by formal entry in the HR system.

### 2.5 Study and Exam Leave
* **Eligibility:** Employees pursuing professional banking certifications or pre-approved degree courses related to their career at Wifak Bank.
* **Entitlement:** Up to **5 paid days per calendar year** to prepare for and sit exams. Documentation proving exam registration must be attached to the request.

### 2.6 Unpaid Leave
* Under exceptional circumstances, employees may request unpaid leave for personal reasons up to a maximum of **3 months**.
* Requests must be reviewed by the Department Head and require final approval from the Chief Human Resources Officer (CHRO).
* During unpaid leave, annual leave accruals are suspended, and company pension contributions are paused.

---

## 3. Policy Governance and Violations
* Unauthorized absence or failure to provide necessary documentation (e.g., medical certificates) may lead to suspension of pay and disciplinary action, up to and including termination.
* Managers are responsible for monitoring team leave balances to ensure operational risk is managed and employees do not accumulate excessive unused leave.
