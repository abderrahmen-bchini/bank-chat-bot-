# Wifak Bank - Corporate Expense Policy

**Document Reference:** FIN-POL-012  
**Effective Date:** March 1, 2026  
**Version:** 4.1  
**Owner:** Finance Department  

---

## 1. Purpose & Core Principles
This policy defines the rules and guidelines governing the reimbursement of business-related expenses incurred by employees on behalf of Wifak Bank. All expenditures must be reasonable, necessary, directly related to bank operations, and compliant with local tax regulations.

---

## 2. Business Travel Guidelines

### 2.1 Air Travel
* **Economy Class:** All domestic flights and international flights under **6 hours** must be booked in Economy Class.
* **Premium Economy / Business Class:** For international flights exceeding **6 hours**, employees may book Premium Economy (or Business Class for Directors and above), subject to written pre-approval from the Department Head.
* **Booking Window:** Flights must be booked through the bank's approved travel agency (*Amex Global Business Travel*) at least **14 days in advance** whenever possible to secure preferred rates.

### 2.2 Lodging & Accommodations
* **Caps:** Hotel bookings must not exceed the following nightly limits (excluding local taxes):
  * **Standard Domestic Cities:** Capped at **$200 / night**.
  * **High-Cost Hubs (New York, London, Tokyo, Geneva):** Capped at **$350 / night**.
* **Room Class:** Only standard single rooms are eligible for reimbursement. Upgrades to suites or executive rooms are the personal financial responsibility of the employee.

### 2.3 Local Transportation
* **Public Transit & Rideshares:** Employees are encouraged to use public transit, trains, or standard ridesharing options (e.g., UberX, Lyft Standard). Premium ride categories (Uber Black, XL) are only reimbursable when traveling in groups of 3 or more with heavy luggage.
* **Rental Cars:** Intermediate or compact cars are the standard selection. GPS, satellite radio, and pre-paid fuel options are non-reimbursable.
* **Personal Vehicle Mileage:** If using a personal vehicle for business travel, employees can claim **$0.67 per mile** (or the current local statutory rate). Commutes to the primary office location are not reimbursable.

---

## 3. Meals and Entertainment

### 3.1 Individual Business Meals
When traveling on bank business, the daily maximum allowance (per diem cap) for meals is **$75 per day**. This can be divided as follows:
* **Breakfast:** Up to **$15**
* **Lunch:** Up to **$20**
* **Dinner:** Up to **$40**
* *Note:* Itemized receipts are required for all meal expenses. Lump-sum claims without receipts will be rejected.

### 3.2 Client Entertainment
* **Pre-Approval:** Client entertainment must have a clear business purpose and requires written pre-approval from a Vice President or above if estimated to exceed **$150 per event**.
* **Documentation:** Expense claims must include a list of all attendees (names, titles, organizations) and a brief description of the business topic discussed.
* **Alcohol:** Reasonable alcohol purchases during client dinners are permitted but must be moderate. Excessive alcohol billing will be flagged and reviewed.

---

## 4. Submission & Reimbursement Process
* **Receipt Requirement:** Itemized receipts are mandatory for all transactions. Credit card slips or bank statements alone are not acceptable proof of purchase.
* **Filing Window:** Expense reports must be submitted via the *ExpenseBot* system within **30 days** of incurring the expense. Late submissions exceeding 60 days will not be reimbursed.
* **Approval Chain:** All reports require approval from the employee's direct manager. Expense reports over **$2,500** also require secondary approval from the Finance Director.
* **Reimbursement Timeline:** Approved expenses are paid directly to the employee's salary bank account within **7 to 10 business days** of final approval.

---

## 5. Non-Reimbursable Expenses
The following items will not be paid or reimbursed by Wifak Bank under any circumstances:
1. Parking tickets, speeding fines, or traffic violations.
2. In-room hotel minibar items or pay-per-view movies.
3. Personal care services (laundry, spa, gym access) for business trips lasting under 5 days.
4. Annual fees for personal credit cards.
5. Travel insurance (covered under the bank's corporate policy).
6. Lost personal property or luggage.
